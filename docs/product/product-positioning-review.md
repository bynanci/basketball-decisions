# R2 Product Positioning & Target User Review

## One-sentence positioning

Court IQ turns basketball video analysis into traceable decision evidence, player value insights, and actionable training plans for coaching staffs.

## Target user ranking (v0.1 priority)

1. **Primary ICP: Coaches (head/assistant coaches and player-development coaches)**
   - They own lineup/game/practice decisions and directly convert evidence into actions.
   - They feel the pain of fragmented clips, subjective debates, and slow translation from film to practice plans.
2. **Secondary: Analysts / video coordinators**
   - They operate workflows, maintain artifact quality, and package outputs for coaches.
   - They are critical enablers but not the ultimate economic buyer for v0.1 value realization.
3. **Tertiary: Players**
   - They consume role-specific feedback, drills, and decision reps generated by coach/analyst workflows.
   - They benefit downstream; they are not the initial workflow owner in v0.1.
4. **Quaternary: Developers / technical evaluators**
   - They are implementation stakeholders, not product ICP.
   - Their role is extending/integrating the stack after positioning is validated.

## Main value proposition

**From one possession or session video, Court IQ produces evidence-linked outputs that help coaches make faster, clearer decisions and turn them into concrete training actions.**

What this means in practice:
- Every recommendation should be traceable back to a specific artifact and reviewable workflow step.
- Outputs are decision-oriented (not just descriptive): player value signals, coaching summaries, and drill/practice suggestions.
- The system is designed to reduce ambiguity and meeting-time debate by making evidence explicit.

## First use case for v0.1 (primary)

**Post-practice film-to-plan loop for coaching staff.**

Minimal v0.1 flow:
1. Ingest a single practice or scrimmage video.
2. Run deterministic analysis and review queue checks.
3. Generate traceable decision/report outputs for priority players or situations.
4. Produce an actionable next-practice plan (drills + focus points) tied to that evidence.

Success criteria for this first use case:
- Coach can explain *why* a recommendation exists (traceability).
- Coach can immediately act on output in the next session (actionability).
- Analyst can re-run/verify flow without hidden manual magic (repeatability).

## Secondary use cases (after primary flow is stable)

- **Player feedback packaging:** role-based takeaways and quiz reps for individual development.
- **Analyst QA/governance workflows:** source quality checks, review actions, and rule-set iteration.
- **Cross-session trend monitoring:** lightweight player value trend views to support coaching conversations.

## Non-goals (what Court IQ should not claim in v0.1)

- Not an autonomous coaching replacement or decision autopilot.
- Not a guaranteed win-probability engine.
- Not a real-time in-game strategy recommender.
- Not a fully automated scouting platform across leagues/teams.
- Not a cloud-scale multi-tenant enterprise analytics suite in this phase.
- Not a medical/performance-injury diagnostic tool.

## Risky claims to avoid

Avoid statements like:
- “AI proves which player is best in every lineup.”
- “Fully objective truth from video with no human interpretation.”
- “Automatic game-winning decisions.”
- “Plug-and-play for every camera setup with no calibration/review.”
- “Production-ready model training platform out of the box.”

Safer alternatives:
- “Decision support with evidence traceability.”
- “Coach- and analyst-in-the-loop workflow.”
- “Actionable recommendations tied to reviewable artifacts.”

## 30-second demo pitch (what demo users should understand immediately)

“In under a minute, Court IQ shows how one video becomes coach-ready decisions: we process footage, surface traceable evidence, summarize player-value and decision signals, and turn that into a concrete training plan. It doesn’t replace coaching judgment—it makes coaching decisions faster, clearer, and easier to act on.”

## Positioning guardrails checklist

Before adding features, confirm each proposal strengthens the primary claim:
- Does it improve coach decision clarity?
- Does it preserve or improve evidence traceability?
- Does it increase training actionability?
- Does it avoid overclaiming automation or predictive certainty?

If “no” to any of the first three, or “yes” to the overclaiming risk, de-prioritize for v0.1.
